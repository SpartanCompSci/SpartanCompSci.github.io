Perfect Smoked Brisket Recipe

Ingredients Instructions Tips

Overview

This recipe will guide you through the process of smoking a mouth-watering brisket, with a rich smoky flavor and tender texture that’s perfect for any barbecue enthusiast. Follow each step to achieve brisket perfection!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef brisket (about 10-12 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup black pepper
  • 1/4 cup paprika
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper (optional for extra spice)
  • Wood chips (oak or hickory recommended for smoking)

Instructions

1. Prepare the Brisket

Trim the excess fat, leaving about 1/4 inch of fat on the top to keep the meat moist. Rub the brisket generously with the salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper.

2. Preheat the Smoker

Set your smoker to 225°F (107°C). Choose your wood chips and allow the smoker to reach the set temperature before placing the brisket inside.

3. Smoke the Brisket

Place the brisket in the smoker with the fat side up. Smoke for about 1.5 hours per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 195°F (90°C). Check periodically to ensure consistent temperature.

4. Wrap the Brisket

Once the brisket reaches an internal temperature of around 160°F (71°C), wrap it in butcher paper or aluminum foil to retain moisture and prevent it from drying out.

5. Rest the Brisket

Remove the brisket from the smoker and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. This allows the juices to redistribute, making it more tender and flavorful.

6. Slice and Serve

Slice the brisket against the grain and serve with your favorite barbecue sauce and sides. Enjoy your perfectly smoked brisket!

Tips for Perfect Brisket

  • Choose the right cut: Look for a whole packer brisket with both the flat and point cuts for a balanced texture.
  • Monitor temperature: Keep a close eye on the smoker temperature and the internal temperature of the brisket to avoid overcooking.
  • Be patient: Brisket takes time. Rushing it will compromise the flavor and tenderness.
  • Use a water pan: Placing a pan of water in the smoker helps maintain humidity and prevents the brisket from drying out.
